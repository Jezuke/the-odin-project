Kare-Kare

Description

Kare-kare. A dish that I loved as a child, despised as a teenager, and fell back in love with as an adult. This stew is rich in flavor from its base ingredient: peanut butter. As a child I enjoyed this dish and it was common in to have it at least once a week in my household, however, when I found out it was made out of peanut butter all of a sudden my appetite for this dish vanished. I was the type of kid who absolutely despised pineapple on pizza and fruit with hearty dishes, so it just didn't sit right with me that peanut butter should be mixed with meat. But through the years I once again fell in love with this dish.

As I became more open to complex flavors and an appreciation of ingredients as a medium for food, that there was no right or wrong way to mix ingredients (debatable), I began to appreciate the rebellious nature of certain ingredients in creating an absolutely delicious meal. There's a lesson to be had in this I guess... Things that don't fit the puzzle at first may have just been oriented wrong as was my perspective on this dish's composition originally.

Ingredients

  • Oxtail
  • Water
  • Eggplants
  • String beans/sitaw
  • Pechay/BokChoy
  • Rice flour
  • Peanut Butter
  • Garlic
  • Onions
  • Annatto Powder
  • Fish Sauce

Steps

  1. Rinse oxtail and trim excess fat. In a deep pot place oxtail and enough water to submerge the meat.
  2. Bring to a boil until oxtail is soft.
  3. Drain some of the water to reserve as broth.
  4. In another pot, bring enough water to boil to submerge sliced eggplant, string beans, and other veggies.
  5. In a skillet over medium heat, add rice flour and cook till lightly brown and toasted.
  6. In a bowl, combine toasted rice flour with some of the reserved broth and whisk till smooth.
  7. In another bowl, combine annatto powder and some of the reserved broth until powder is dissolved.
  8. In another bowl, combine peanut butter and some of the reserved broth and mix till well-blended.
  9. In a large pot, heat oil and saut√© onions and garlic till golden brown. Add oxtail and cook until lightly brown, then add fish sauce and cook for 2 to 3 minutes
  10. Add annatto paste, rice flour mixture, and peanut butter mixture with whatever remaining broth you have and mix.
  11. Season with salt and pepper to taste and simmer till texture thickens.
  12. Add vegetables and cook till tender.
  13. Serve hot and enjoy!
